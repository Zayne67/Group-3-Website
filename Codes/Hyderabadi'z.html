Hyderabadi'z


By: Nicolas Stanley M Parilla

Hyderabadi cuisine also known as Deccani cuisine of India, was developed after the foundation of Qutb shahidynasty by Sultan Quli, promoting the native cuisine along with their own.
Hyderabadi cuisine had become a princely legacy of the Nizams ofHyderabad State. It is an amalgamation of Mughlai, Turkish and Arabic along with the influence of the native Telugu and Marathwada cuisines.
Hyderabadi cuisine comprises a broad repertoire of rice, wheat and meat dishes and the skilled use of various spices, herbs and natural edibles.
